A domain name is the common tongue name of a website. For example, google.com is the domain name for the Google search website. A domain name can be any combination of letters and numbers and ends with a domain name extension such as “.com”, “.org” and many others. Each domain name actually references a specific IP address, but IP addresses are strings of numbers and letters which are difficult to remember and work with for humans hence the need for the cross reference to domain names. Domain names must be registered and follow the DNS (Domain Name System) rules and procedures when they are created. You can register a new domain from domain name registrars such as godaddy.com. According to Business Standard India, 330.6 million domain names have been registered in 2017.

ICANN stands for the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers. They are the authorizing body for domain name registrars such as godaddy.com. It is an American non-profit company founded in 1998. They also govern DNS and they are responsible for the IPv4 and IPv6 protocols. Finally, they manage top level domains as well. Top levels domains are the few characters in a domain name such as .com, .can, .net, etc.. It is easy to see that ICANN is a very important governing body in the modern internet.
